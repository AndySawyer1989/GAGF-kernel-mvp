\# GAGF Constitution



\*\*Version:\*\* 1.0

\*\*Status:\*\* Ratified for GAGF Kernel MVP v0.1.0



\---



\# Preamble



The General Adaptive Governance Framework (GAGF) exists to support evidence-based governance through deterministic reasoning, transparent policy execution, and accountable human oversight.



This Constitution defines the principles that govern every architectural, engineering, and product decision within the platform.



If an implementation conflicts with this Constitution, the implementation must be revised.



\---



\# Article I — Reality is Primary



Reality is the source of truth.



The platform does not create reality.



It observes, records, and reasons about reality through evidence.



\---



\# Article II — Evidence Precedes Inference



Evidence must exist before conclusions are drawn.



Evidence shall be:



\* observable

\* attributable

\* replayable

\* auditable



Inference is derived from evidence—not the reverse.



\---



\# Article III — Deterministic Governance



Given identical:



\* evidence

\* policy

\* adaptive state



the Kernel shall produce the same governance decision.



Determinism is a foundational property of the GAGF Kernel.



\---



\# Article IV — Explainability



Every governance decision must be explainable.



Each decision must identify:



\* the governing policy

\* the supporting evidence

\* the adaptive state

\* the resulting strategy

\* the confidence associated with the evidence



No decision should exist without an explanation.



\---



\# Article V — Human Accountability



Humans retain responsibility for governance.



The platform assists.



The Kernel governs.



Humans remain accountable for decisions that affect organizations, systems, or people.



\---



\# Article VI — AI is Advisory



Artificial Intelligence serves as an advisory capability.



AI may:



\* explain

\* summarize

\* query

\* simulate

\* recommend

\* generate reports



AI shall not directly modify:



\* governance policy

\* Kernel arbitration

\* evidence records

\* Snapshot Ledger

\* Decision Ledger



Recommendations require human review before adoption.



\---



\# Article VII — Policy Integrity



Governance Policy Language (GPL) shall be versioned.



Policy evolution must be explicit, reviewable, and traceable.



Historical policies remain reproducible.



\---



\# Article VIII — Evidence Preservation



Evidence is append-only.



Evidence shall never be silently rewritten or destroyed.



Historical replay is a core capability of the platform.



\---



\# Article IX — Separation of Responsibilities



The platform shall maintain clear architectural boundaries.



Examples include:



\* Evidence Layer

\* Kernel Layer

\* Service Layer

\* API Layer

\* Presentation Layer

\* Intelligence Layer



Each layer should have a well-defined responsibility.



\---



\# Article X — Evolution Through Validation



The platform evolves through observation, testing, and evidence.



New governance strategies, policies, and AI capabilities should be validated before adoption.



Speculation is not a substitute for evidence.



\---



\# Guiding Principle



\*\*Reality is observed.\*\*



\*\*Evidence is preserved.\*\*



\*\*The Kernel governs.\*\*



\*\*AI advises.\*\*



\*\*Humans are accountable.\*\*
